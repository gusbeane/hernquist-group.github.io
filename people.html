People

Lars is a primary advisor for N Graduate students and M Postdoctoral Fellows.

Ph.D. Students

Magdalena Siwek

Magdalena (G-6) develops numerical simulations of circumbinary disks (CBDs), and study the effect of CBD driven binary evolution on EM transients and GW source populations.

Angus "Gus" Beane

Gus (G-5) works on controlled simulations of galaxies, with a particular focus on understanding the formation and evolution of the Milky Way.

Ana Maria Delgado

Ana Maria's (G-4) interests lie in cosmological simulations and Machine Learning.

Mouza Almualla

Mouza (G-2) is studying Line intensity mapping and Lyman alpha forest using THESAN simulations.

Olga Borodina

Olga (G-2) runs hydrodynamical simulations of jets in the galactic center that propogate through interstellar medium and cause galaxy quenching.

PostDocs

Aaron Smith

A major theme of Aaron's research is to understand the nature of the first galaxies and the ensuing cosmic phase transition known as the Epoch of Reionization

David Guszejnov

David runs large-scale magnetohydrodynamic simulations of molecular clouds to find out how stars are born.

Paola Domínguez Fernández

Paola's lines of research are cosmic magnetism (origin and evolution of magnetic fields in the Universe) and non-thermal phenomenae in galaxy clusters (particle acceleration mechanisms and magnetic field amplification in the intracluster medium).

Vadim Semenov

Vadim designs and analyzes supercomputer simulations of galaxies to gain insights into how galaxies form and evolve; in particular, how galactic disks form, why star formation is inefficient, as well as a variety of other topics in computational galaxy formation.

Jake Bennett

Jake works on galaxy formation simulations, particularly focussing on the gaseous haloes around galaxies and the growth of early galaxies (and their black holes) in the Universe.

Yueying Ni

Yueying mainly works on cosmological simulations of galaxy formation, studying the growth, feedback and coalescence of massive black holes.
